What does Success mean to me?

Being successful doesn’t necessarily mean becoming rich or wealthy. Success means a lot of different things to different people. To some people, it is establishing a family. To others, it may mean money or fame.

There’s no limited range of meanings, it depends on the person’s values and the way they define it. There’s no wrong definition. There’re also no limited actions to be considered successful. You don’t have to own a big business or to be a superstar. Sometimes, little things achieved can make the most successful person of oneself.

That’s exactly what I’ve learned from my life experience. I’ve become grateful. I appreciate every single achievement no matter how big it is. I look at myself proudly every time I accomplish little goals I set to myself. You might be wondering what my little goals are! To put you in the picture, they’re not special goals (I still have special ones). Sometimes I reward myself because I got a 100 on a programming assignment! Other times, I would be so happy because I learned something new.

Appreciating little thing you’ve done can make you successful. It also doesn’t mean that I don’t want to become wealthy or have my own business. The idea is that a lot of people don’t realize that they’ve done enough to be considered successful. It is you who decide who you are!

Most of my life has been dedicated to giving back to community, and I consider this to be the biggest quality that makes me successful. Why? Because it is always the opportunity to improve someone’s life. Being a certified trainer, many trainees reached out to me saying that I was the reason they chose their field of study or they’ve become better time managers, … etc. These things mean A LOT to me. It makes me feel that I made an impact.

One of the things that made me feel happy is that a high school student, who’s attending the same school I graduated from in 2014, reached out to me and told me that my name is still mentioned by teachers and the dean. It’s amazing when you become an influencer without even realizing! Therefore, I always say that the greatest success is not only through achieving objectives but through helping others to succeed. Being memorable for good stuff you did is enough to make you a great person!


Now, I aspire to help women in my community succeed in the technical field. My passion for software engineering and computer science is huge, and I want to see more women having the same passion. It’s part of giving back to community. If you’re wondering why would I do this? It’s because it pisses me off to see few women attending a CS class among a lot of men or to hear people stereotyping women and underestimate their technical skills. Gender has nothing to do with how skillful and good you are! Stop gender criticism!

To conclude, don’t let anything discourage you from giving back to your community and being successful! For women, don’t let any single thing discourage you from considering a degree in the technical field. You are strong, capable, and hard work. If you put something in your mind, I know, you’ll achieve it no matter what. .
